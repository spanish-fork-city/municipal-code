3.04.010 Claims Against The City In General

Every person asserting a claim against the City shall be required to comply in all respects with the provisions of the Utah Governmental Immunity Act, Utah Code Ann. §63-30-1 et seq. Any such claim received by the City shall be submitted to Utah Risk Management Mutual Association for review and handling. Any claimant shall be required to provide such additional information as may be requested by URMMA. Any claim referred back to the City by URMMA, on the basis that the City lacks coverage for that particular claim, shall be referred to the City council for review and disposal. The City council shall have full authority to settle and/or litigate any such claim.

3.08.010 Definitions

For the purpose of this Title, the following terms, phrases, words, and their derivations shall have the meanings given herein:

"Bid Pack" Documents executed between the City and contractor setting forth the obligations of said parties, including but not limited to performance, labor, equipment and materials, and basis of payment. These documents include: Notice of Invitation to Bid, Information for Bidders, Bid Cover Page, Bid Bond, Bid Agreement, General Conditions, Special Provisions, Payment and Performance Bonds, Notice of Award, Notice to Proceed, Bid Schedule, Drawings prepared for or by City and any Addenda.

"Change Order" A request to change the scope of a project. Change orders may change the overall project cost unless they include a contingency reduction. Change orders may extend the project deadline.

"City" Spanish Fork City, incorporated January 19, 1855.

"City’s Bidders List" A maintained list of contractors, vendors, and consultants with the City from whom bids or proposals can be solicited. Said list shall be used by the City to notify potential bidders.

"City Manager" The Spanish Fork City Manager or his/her designee.

"Construction Management/General Contractor (CM/GC) Contract" The City may contract with the construction manager early in a project to assist in the development of a cost-effective design. In a Construction Manager/General Contractor (CM/GC) contract, the CM/GC becomes the general contractor and is at risk for the responsibilities of a general contractor for the project, including meeting the specifications, complying with applicable laws, rules, and regulations, that the project will be completed on time and will not exceed a specified maximum price.

"Contingency Request" A request to use project contingency funds. May not increase the scope of the project or extend the project deadline.

"Design-Build" Design-Build is a form of project delivery where the City contracts, under a single contract, with one contractor or consultant (a design-builder) to provide and take contractual responsibility for both the design services and the construction services.

"Electronic Bid Page" A digital platform or webpage used by the City to facilitate the process of submitting bids or proposals electronically for various projects, contracts, or procurement opportunities.

"Finance Director" The Spanish Fork City Finance Director or his/her designee.

"Lowest Responsible Bidder" Person or firm who has the capability in all respects to fully perform the contract requirements and who has the integrity and reliability which will assure good faith performance. The lowest responsible bidder is the bidder who has submitted the lowest bid to furnish supplies or contractual services to the City that meets the standards set forth in this definition. The lowest responsible bidder's bid shall comply with the specifications, delivery terms and conditions, and other qualifications and requirements included in the invitation for bids, and shall be accompanied by any bonds required by the City or other applicable law. In determining the lowest responsible bidder, the City shall give primary emphasis to bid price, but may also consider the following items in addition to the actual bid price:

The ability, capacity, experience, past performance and skill of the bidder to perform the service required.

Whether the bidder can perform the contract or provide his or her services within the time specified.

The quality and performance of previous services by the bidder, either to the City or another entity.

Quality, availability and adaptability of the supplies or contractual services to the particular use required.

The ability of the bidder to provide future maintenance and service.

The number and scope of conditions attached to the bid or price quotation.

The maintenance history of the product, the parts and service costs of the product, existing inventory, mechanic's expertise, and ease of maintenance.

All bidders shall furnish information and data requested by the City that will assist the City in determining whether or not a particular bidder is the "lowest responsible bidder".

"Maximum Fee" The maximum allowable payment for a contract established by this chapter.

"Owner" The Department Director or their designee representing the City.

"Professional Services" means, but is not limited to, the following: auditing, architecture, banking, insurance, engineering, appraisals, legal services, design-build, and other consulting services.

"Professional Service Agreement and Task Orders" Documents executed between the City and consultant setting forth the obligations of said parties, including but not limited to performance, labor, equipment and materials, and basis of payment.

These documents include agreement, scope of services, fee schedule and a not to exceed amount.

"Responsible Bidder" An offer, submitted by a contractor, vendor or consultant to furnish supplies, equipment or contractual services in conformity with the specifications, delivery terms and conditions, and other requirements included in the invitation for bids.

"UDOT’s Qualified Pools" Each pool is a list of prequalified consultants or contractors in specific work disciplines.

3.08.030 Choice Of Purchasing - Bidding Process

The purchasing process options below may be used according to the following costs. Recurring purchases and subscriptions shall be based on the estimated annual cost. The cost of the supply or service shall not be divided to avoid purchasing process requirements.

A. $15,000 or Less. For purchases and contracts of $15,000 or less, the City need not follow any bidding procedure, but shall seek to obtain the best value possible.

B. $15,000 to $75,000. For purchases and contracts more than $15,000 and less than $75,000, the City must follow one of the following bid processes when possible.

1. Sole Source

2. Cooperative Bidding

3. Interlocal Cooperative Bidding

4. Direct Select

5. Informal Competitive Bidding

6. Request for Proposals

7. Formal Competitive Bidding

C. $75,000 or More. For purchases and contracts that cost $75,000 or more, the City must follow one of the following bid processes when possible.

1. Sole Source

2. Cooperative Bidding

3. Interlocal Cooperative Bidding

4. Request for Proposals

5. Formal Competitive Bidding

6. Construction Management and General Contracting (CMGC)

In cases where more than one alternative is listed as acceptable for a given dollar amount, any of the listed alternatives shall be acceptable, and the City shall not incur any liability for choosing one alternative over another.

3.08.040 Purchasing - Bidding Processes

Sole Source. Sole Source is the process where one contractor, vendor, or consultant is capable of providing the required product or service as determined by the City by conducting a market survey and Direct Select or by an original bidding procurement process.

Sole Source purchasing may only be made for goods or services listed on the Sole Source Vendor and Product List. The Finance Director maintains this list. This list shall include the following:

Vendor Name, Contact, and Contact Information

Product Name

Estimated Annual Cost of Product

Written Justification for Sole Source

Special Instructions

Application Date

Expiration Date

Link to Contract if Applicable

Purchasers must use the Sole Source Application Form to apply to have a vendor and product on this list. Invoices from sole source vendors may be approved if vendor and product is on the Sole Source Vendor and Product List and the invoice date is before the Expiration Date. The Expiration Date may only be up to 5 years after the Application Date after which a purchaser must reapply for sole source. The Finance Director and City Manager are authorized to sign Sole Source contracts.

Cooperative Bidding. Cooperative Bidding is the process in which the City purchases goods or services from vendors on the Approved Cooperative Bidding Systems List. This list is maintained by the Finance Director.

Interlocal Cooperative Bidding. Interlocal Cooperative Bidding is the process of using competitive bids from another public agency within the state for the purchase of any goods or services. The City shall make a separate contract with the contractor, vendor, or consultant for the goods or services provided at the bid price. The original bid and bid tabulation shall be included as an appendix to the contract. The City contract quantity must be of similar or lesser amount than the original bid quantity.

Direct Select. This process is used to select a contractor, vendor, or consultant without obtaining other bids, advertising, or making a public notice.

Selected contractor, vendor, or consultant must be on the City Bidders List or UDOT’s Qualified Pools.

Selected contractor, vendor, or consultant must provide a proposal with the following.

Schedule

Separate schedules for sub-tasks and alternate supplies or work if needed. All consulting work shall include sub-task schedules.

Detailed description of everything included with each line item.

Time and material proposals shall also include the following. All consulting work shall submit time and material proposals.

Equipment and personnel billing rates with estimated hours in each sub-task.

Detailed description of specific equipment and personnel in each sub-task.

Sub-tasks totals shall add up to the Maximum Fee.

G. Formal Competitive Bidding. In this process, contractors, vendors or consultants are invited to submit formal sealed bids to provide a designated product or service in accordance with specifications provided by the City.

b. Post notice on the Utah Public Notice Website at least seven days before opening the bids.

c. Deliver to all known responsible prospective bidders, including those whose names are on the City Bidders List.

d. Post on the City Electronic Bid Page.

Sealed bids (or electronically submitted bids) shall be submitted as designated in the notice with the statement "Bid for (item or project)" on the envelope (or in the electronic transmission).

Bids shall be opened in public at the time and place stated in the notice.

A tabulation of all bids received shall be open for public inspection during regular business hours for a period of not less than thirty (30) days after the bid opening.

Bids shall be awarded or rejected as set forth in Section 3.08.060.

H. Construction Management and General Contracting (CM/GC). This Process is an integrated approach to planning, design, and construction. City, designer(s), and contractor(s) work collaboratively to develop the project scope, optimize the design, improve quality, manage costs and share risks. Project delivery method consists of two phases-design and construction. When the City considers the design to be complete the contractor then has the opportunity to bid on the project based on the completed design and schedule. Except as otherwise specified here, all provisions outlined in the City’s 3.08 Purchasing Policy shall apply to said contract.

1. Goal. The objective of CM/GC (Construction Manager/General Contractor) should prioritize equitable treatment for all involved parties, ensuring fairness and equality, while streamlining design and procurement activities. It aims to foster effective, wide-reaching competition. The City seeks optimal value, aiming for on-time project completion, adherence to budget, maintaining high-quality standards, and ensuring safe construction practices.

2. Development of Project Goals. The Project Team should identify goals in coordination with an oversight committee comprised of an elected official, department director, finance director, city engineer, project manager and any other appropriate personnel required. These goals should be concise and generally based on scope, schedule, budget, quality and impacts to the public.

3. Develop a Request for Proposal (RFP) and Evaluation Manual. Use the RFP method outlined in 3.08.040(F). Generally, the CM/GC RFP Template shall be used

4. Procurement. CM/GC shall procure its first tier subcontractors using one of the source selection methods outlined in this Policy.

5.Guaranteed Maximum Price (GMP). The CM/GC shall provide the City with a GMP which is the final price that the CM/GC agrees to accept in full performance of a Construction Manager/General Contractor Agreement (CM/GC Agreement) and is based on the final contract drawings and specifications. The GMP shall include all fees and percentages required by this RFP, as well as the costs for general conditions and all work as required in the Contract Documents.

Work may be completed in project phases and separate bid packages. The successful CM/GC will be required to submit a GMP for each of these phases and packages. The sum or total of all the phases shall be the final GMP. The final GMP is normally determined at the completion of the contract documents and receipt of subcontractor’s bids. However, a GMP may be negotiated at an earlier point as may be needed by the City.

The CM/GC is not allowed to proceed into construction unless the City agrees that the GMP provided, as part of a guarantee to complete the project, or a portion of the project, is fair and reasonable. If the City determines it is in its best interest and/or that the GMP is not fair or reasonable, the city may put the project out to a formal bid. The contractor's GMP shall be used as their submitted bid price.

F. CM/GC GMP Contingency. At the sole discretion of the City, during the finalization of the GMP, a specific sum of funds may be earmarked for inclusion in the GMP as a GMP Contingency. This allocation will be clearly defined within the Final GMP, specifically tailored to address elements of work identified in that particular GMP.

The CM/GC GMP Contingency is intended for the following:

1. Design or modifications not represented in construction documents used as the basis of fee (e.g., costs arising from design alterations between 90% design completion and the CM/GC contract award);

2. Quantity Overruns;

3. Minor design changes authorized by the City;

4. Identified Risks;

5. Shared Risk Items.

The CM/GC must indicate on the CM/GC Project Cost Form their proposed percentage of the Project construction costs for this CM/GC GMP Contingency (e.g., 5%, 10%, etc.). All unused portions of the CM/GC GMP Contingency shall revert back to the City.

G. Owner-controlled Contingency. At the sole discretion of the City, an overall Project budgeting reserve (a.k.a. Owner-controlled Contingency) may be held and maintained exclusive from the CM/GC Project costs and GMP. This Owner-controlled Contingency, if held, will be in an amount, determined by the City, and inclusive in the overall Project budget, to properly account for potential increased cost due to changes in the work, made at the discretion of the City, that were not anticipated by the City and are beyond the control of the City and or the CM/GC at the start of the program and for possible cost escalation(s), if any.

The Owner-controlled Contingency is a fund allocation intended to cover the following:

1. Owner-directed design or scope changes;

2. Design errors or omissions;

3. Unforeseen conditions not noted in CM/GC GMP Contingency;

4. Possible cost escalations, if any.

The City will indicate a proposed percentage of the Project construction costs for this Owner-controlled Contingency.

H. Insurance and Bonds. As a contract is awarded and then proceeds, the CM/GC must furnish the City with all appropriate Bonds and Insurance as required by the Contract Documents, including but not limited to appropriate insurance, Bid Bond(s), Performance Bond(s), Payment Bond(s), etc.

I. Contract Documents. CM/GC Contract Template shall be used and must be approved by the City.

3.08.050 Awarding Bids, Purchases, Contracts, Task Orders, And Change Orders

Selected contractor, vendor, or consultant must be added to the City Bidders List before a contract is awarded.

Appeals. Any contractor, vendor, or consultant who is aggrieved in connection with the solicitation or award of a contract may appeal to the Finance Director. An appeal with respect to an invitation for bids or an RFP shall be submitted in writing prior to the opening of bids or the closing date for proposals, unless the aggrieved person did not know and should not have known of the facts giving rise to the protest prior to the bid opening or the closing date for proposals. The appeal shall be submitted in writing within five working days after the aggrieved person knows or should have known of the facts giving rise thereto. The appeal shall be handled pursuant to policies established by the Finance Director. The Finance Director shall promptly issue a written decision regarding the appeal. The written decision shall state the reasons for the action taken. The Finance Director’s decision shall be deemed final unless overturned by a Court of competent jurisdiction. The statute of limitations set forth in Section 63-56-817 of the Utah Procurement Code shall apply to any suits filed related to the application of this Article, with the date of the Finance Director’s decision substituted for the date of the final administrative decision.

Budget. Budgeted bids, purchases, or contracts may be awarded by the Finance Director unless they exceed the budget by more than $35,000 or 10% of the approved budget amount, whichever is greater. When expenditures exceed this threshold, approval must be sought from the City Council. Projects with payments spanning multiple budget years require City Council approval unless future fiscal year budget allocations for the project are explicitly noted in the approved budget.

Bonds. Payment and performance bonds are required for construction projects costing more than $75,000. The City shall have authority to require any other bonds deemed necessary in such amounts as deemed necessary to protect the interests of the City. The types and amounts of the bonds to be required shall be described in the notice inviting bids.

Change Orders. The maximum allowable markup for overhead and profit for a change order is 10%. Change orders which increase the contract price in an amount less than the greater of $35,000 or 10% of the contract amount may be approved by the City Finance Director provided further that the sum of all change orders do not increase the contract price by more than the greater of $50,000 or 15% of the contract amount.

Form Documents. All forms and documents utilized within the framework of this policy must receive approval through an administrative order.

Insurance. Contractor, vendor, or consultant shall have all the required insurance. Builders risk insurance shall be required on all building types of construction.

Licenses. Contractor, vendor, or consultant shall have all the required licenses.

Lowest Responsible Bidder. Except as otherwise allowed or required, the City shall award the contract or bid to the lowest responsible bidder.

Negotiation of Bids. Where a bid exceeds available funds and time or economic considerations preclude re-solicitation of work or purchase of a reduced scope or quantity, the City Finance Director may negotiate an adjustment of the bid price, including changes in the bid requirements, with the lowest responsible bidder, in order to bring the low bid within the amount of available funds.

Rejection of Bids. The City Finance Director may, in his or her own discretion, reject any and all bids presented, and may re-advertise for bids as set forth in this Article.

Single Bids. The City Finance Director may require a price or cost analysis if only one bid is received. The bidder may be required to furnish a detailed cost proposal, and the bid award shall be subject to subsequent negotiation.

Tie Bids. If two (2) or more of the bids received are for the same total amount or list price, quality and service being equal, the City Finance Director may negotiate with the bidders and obtain the best bid possible and/or give a preference to a local bidder.

3.08.100 Exceptions To Bidding Requirements

Auction, Closeout, Bankruptcy Sales. If the Finance Director determines that supplies, materials or equipment can be purchased at any public auction, closeout sale, bankruptcy sale or other similar sale, and if the Finance Director finds that a purchase at any such auction or sale will be made at a cost below the market cost in the county, a contract or contracts may be let, or the purchase made, without complying with the competitive bidding requirements of this Chapter

Contracts Not Suited to Competitive Bidding. Contracts, which by their nature are not suited to award by competitive bidding as determined by the Finance Director, shall not be subject to the competitive bidding requirements. These contracts include, but are not necessarily limited to:

Sole Source. Sole source procurement may be used only if the Finance Director determines that a service, product, or requirement is reasonably available only from a single supplier or contractor. Examples of circumstances which may necessitate sole source procurement are:

If there is only one vendor or supplier who can provide the needed product or service;

If the compatibility of supplies, accessories, replacement parts, or service is the paramount consideration;

If a sole supplier’s product is needed for trial use or testing;

If the City is procuring utility services;

If the City seeks to add to, repair or maintain supplies owned by the City which may be more efficiently added to, repaired or maintained by a particular person or firm; and

If the City seeks supplies which, by reason of the training of City personnel or the inventory of replacement parts maintained by the City, is more compatible with the existing supplies owned by the City and if the supplies can only be acquired from a sole source.

Equipment. Contracts for equipment which, by reason of the training of City personnel or the inventory of replacement parts maintained by the City, is more compatible with the existing equipment owned by the City.

Equipment Additions, Repairs, and Maintenance. Contracts for additions to, repairs, and maintenance of equipment owned by the City which may be more efficiently added to, repaired or maintained by a particular person or firm.

Donated Funds. In cases where a donor or grantor has contributed funds to the City, the City may expend the funds in the manner designated by the donor or grantor in lieu of the procedures set forth in this ordinance.

Emergency Purchases. In times of emergency, the Finance Director may choose the most practical means available to procure needed goods and services. If the situation allows, the Finance Director shall attempt to achieve as much competition in the emergency procurement process as possible. Emergency procurement shall be limited to the services, supplies, and construction necessary to meet the emergency. For purposes of this paragraph, an emergency is a condition or situation which creates an immediate threat to the public health, safety, or welfare. Examples of conditions or situations which may constitute emergencies include earthquakes, floods, terrorist attacks, wars, epidemics, riots, road failures, utility failures, structure failures, and equipment failures.

Exchanges. Exchanges of supplies, material or equipment between the City and any other public agency which are not by sale or auction shall be by mutual agreement of the respective public agencies.

Federal or State Money. In cases where federal or state money is being used, or in cases where federal or state procurement laws or procedures govern the types of goods or services being procured, the City shall follow the applicable federal or state procurement laws or procedures in lieu of the procedures set forth in this ordinance.

Ongoing Construction Work. If a contractor has been hired to work in a given area by a person or entity other than the City, and if the City has work that needs to be completed in the area, and if the Finance Director determines that there is an established price for the type of work that the City needs completed and that the City can save on mobilization costs by hiring the contractor that is already in the area, then the City may negotiate with and contract directly with the contractor without following formal purchasing processes.

Specific Professional Service Agreements and Task Orders.

The following Professional Services are exempt from the bidding requirements: auditing, banking, legal services, and Design-Build. These contracts shall be awarded based on professional qualifications, service ability, cost of service, and other criteria deemed important by the City.

A professional services contract for the annual fiscal year financial audit shall only be awarded by the City Council. This contract may be awarded for multiple years. The performance of the auditing firm may be reviewed periodically to determine whether to continue the contract or put it back out to bid.

Contracts amended or extended from the original contract not ethically suited for bidding as determined by the Finance Director.

Contracts suited for a specific contractor, vendor, or consultant specializing in ,that specific area, and that it is found by the Finance Director to be unethical to place out to bid.

Projects Performed by City Employees. City employees may be used to complete City projects, provided that the City complies with State statutory requirements governing contracts for building improvements and public works projects.

Real Estate and Right-of-Way (ROW) acquisitions. Real Estate and ROW purchase will adhere to state laws, city procedure and require oversight and approval from the City Council. Transactions below $100,000 may be approved by the City Manager.

Unsuccessful Formal Competitive Bidding. If the bids received in response to a competitive sealed bid process are unreasonable, noncompetitive, or in excess of available funds, and if the Finance Director determines that time or other circumstances will not permit the delay required to re-solicit competitive sealed bids, the City may procure the needed goods or services using the emergency procurement procedure or any other bidding process listed in Section 3.08.040 Purchasing - Bidding Processes.

Used Supplies and Materials. The City may acquire used supplies without following formal purchasing procedures if the Finance Director determines that there is an established market price for the used supplies and that it is beneficial for the City to acquire the used supplies.

Utah Correctional Industries Division. Goods and services produced by the Utah Correctional Industries Division may be purchased from the Utah Correctional Industries Division without following any of the bidding requirements set forth herein.

City Warehouse Purchases. The City may purchase items from the City Warehouse without following purchasing procedures.

In cases where the error has a material effect on the outcome of the bid process, the Finance Director may;

Reject all bids and rebid with corrections made.

Award the bid to the lowest responsive responsible bidder. The City may elect to disqualify bidders who do not strictly comply with the bid specifications.

3.08.120 Penalties

It is unlawful:

For any bidder or prospective bidder, or any employee or officer thereof, in restraint of freedom of competition or otherwise, by agreement with any other person, bidder, or prospective bidder, to bid a fixed price, or to "rotate" bidding practices among competitors.

For any person to offer or to give to any elected official, officer or employee of the City or any member of his immediate family, any gift, whether in the form of money, services, loan, travel, entertainment, hospitality, promise, or other form, under circumstances in which it could reasonably be intended to influence him/her or could reasonably be expected to influence him/her in his/her duties concerning the award of any contract or order of purchase, or for any elected official, officer or employee to directly or indirectly solicit or directly or indirectly accept any such gift for such purpose.

For any elected official, officer, or employee to disclose, in advance of the opening of the bids, the content of any bid invited through the formal competitive bidding procedure.

For any elected official, officer, or employee to actively participate in the awarding of a contract from which s/he will directly benefit, without fully disclosing any interest s/he has therein.

For any elected official, officer, or employee or other person to appropriate for personal or private use any item of public property unless otherwise authorized by policy.

To purchase supplies or equipment for the personal use of any elected official, officer or employee unless the item or items are required parts of a worker's equipment and are necessary to the successful performance of the duties of such official, officer or employee. Other personal purchases shall not be permitted and will be cause for disciplinary action.

The following contracts are, in the discretion of the City Manager, voidable:

Contracts which result from a conflict of interest under this Chapter or other applicable law.

Contracts awarded to a person or firm that tried to influence the award of such contract by offering something of value to any elected official, officer or employee.

A violation of this section by a City employee shall be cause for disciplinary action. A violation of this section by an elected official shall be malfeasance in office.

The City Council shall create and maintain said special improvement guaranty fund by appropriations from the general fund or by the levy of a tax not to exceed one mill in any one year or by the issuance of general obligation bonds or by appropriation from such other sources as may be determined by the City Council to provide the money necessary for that purpose. Said fund shall be held by the City Treasurer and shall be kept by him or her separate and apart from all other funds held by him or her. (Ord. No. 18-09, Amended 11/03/2009)

3.20.020 Purpose

The 48th Session of the Utah Legislature of Utah has authorized the counties and municipalities of the State of Utah to enact sales and use tax ordinances imposing a one percent tax.

It is the purpose of this ordinance to conform the Sales and Use Tax of the municipality to the requirements of the Sales and Utah Tax Act, Utah Code Annotated., 1953.

3.20.030 Effective Date

This ordinance shall become effective as of 12:01 o'clock a.m., January 1, 1990.

3.20.040 Sales And Use Tax

From and after the effective date of this ordinance, there is levied and there shall be collected and paid a tax upon every retail sale of tangible personal property, services and meals made within the municipality at the rate of one percent.

An excise tax is hereby imposed on the storage, use, or other consumption in this municipality of tangible personal property from any retailer on or after the operative date of this ordinance at the rate of one percent of the sales price of the property.

For the purpose of this ordinance all retail sales shall be presumed to have been consummated at the place of business delivered by the retailer or his or her agent to an out of state destination or to a common carrier for delivery to an out of state destination. In the event a retailer has no permanent place of business, the place or places at which the retail sales are consummated shall be as determined under the rules and regulations prescribed by and adopted by the State Tax Commission. Except as hereinafter provided, and except insofar as they are inconsistent with the provisions of the Sales and Use Tax Act, all of the provisions of Chapter 12, Title 59, Utah Code Annotated, and in force and affect on the effective date of this ordinance, insofar as they relate to sales taxes, excepting §59-12-101 and §59-12-119 thereof, are hereby adopted and made a part of the ordinance as though fully set forth herein.

Wherever, and to the extent that in Chapter 12 of Title 59, Utah Code Annotated, the State of Utah is named or referred to as the taxing agency, the name of this municipality shall be substituted therefore. Nothing in subparagraph (b) shall be deemed to require substitution of the name of the municipality for the word "State" when that word is used as part of the title of the State Tax Commission, or of the Constitution of the State of Utah, nor shall the name of the municipality be substituted for that of the State in any section when the result of that substitution would require action to be taken by or against the municipality or any agency thereof, rather than by or against the State Tax Commission in performing the functions incident to the administration or operation of the ordinance.

If an annual license has been issued to a retailer under Utah Code Annotated §59-12-106, an additional license shall not be required by reason of this section.

There shall be excluded from the purchase price paid or changed by which the tax is measured:

The amount of any sales or use tax imposed by the State of Utah upon a retailer or consumer;

The gross receipts from the sale of or the cost of storage, use or other consumption of tangible personal property upon which a sales or use tax has become due by reason of the sale transaction to any other municipality and any county in the State of Utah, under the sales or use tax ordinance enacted by that county or municipality in accordance with the Sales and Use Tax Act.

The taxable energy brought into the state by a nonresident for the nonresident's own personal use or enjoyment while within the state, except a taxable energy purchased for use in the state by a nonresident living or working in the state at the time of purchase;

3.36.010 Definitions

As used in this chapter, the following terms shall be defined as follows:

“Commission” means the State Tax Commission.

Subject to Subsections (B)(2) and (3), “customer” means the person who is obligated under a contract with a telecommunications provider to pay for telecommunications service received under the contract.

For purposes of this chapter, “customer” means:

the person who is obligated under a contract with a telecommunications provider to pay for telecommunications service received under the contract; or

if the end user is not the person described in Subsection (B)(2)(a), the end user of telecommunications service.

“Customer” does not include a reseller:

of telecommunications service; or

for mobile telecommunications service, of a serving carrier under an agreement to serve the customer outside the telecommunications providers licensed service area.

“End user” means the person who uses a telecommunications service.

For purposes of telecommunications service provided to a person who is not an individual, “end user” means the individual who uses the telecommunications service on behalf of the person who is provided the telecommunications service.

“Gross Receipts attributed to the municipality” means those gross receipts form a transaction for telecommunications services that is located within the municipality for the purposes of sales and use taxes under Utah Code Title 59, Chapter 12, Sales and Use Tax Act and determined in accordance with Utah Code section §59-12-207.

“Gross receipts from telecommunications service” means the revenue that a telecommunications provider receives for telecommunications service rendered except for the amounts collected or paid as:

a tax, fee, or charge:

imposed by a governmental entity;

separately identified as a tax, fee, or charge in the transaction with the customer for the telecommunications service; and

imposed only on a telecommunications provider;

sales and use taxes collected by the telecommunications provider from a customer under Title 59, Chapter 12. Sales and Use Tax Act; or

interest, a fee, or a charge that is charged by a telecommunications provider on a customer for failure to pay for telecommunications service when payment is due.

“Mobile telecommunications service” is as defined in the Mobile Telecommunications Sourcing Act, 4 U.S.C. Sec. 124.

“Municipality” means Spanish Fork City.

“Place of primary use”;

for telecommunications service other than mobile telecommunications service, means the street address representative of where the customer's use of the telecommunications service primarily occurs, which shall be;

the residential street address of the customer; or

the primary business street address of the customer; or

for mobile telecommunications service, as defined in the Mobile Telecommunications Sourcing Act, 4 U.S.C. Sec. 124.

Notwithstanding where a telephone call is billed or paid, "service address" means:

if the location described in the Subsection (I)(1) is not known, the location of the telecommunications equipment:

to which a call is charged; and

from which the call originates or terminates;

if the location described in Subsection (I)(1) is not known but the location described in this Subsection (I)(2) is known, the location of the origination point of the signal of the telecommunications service first identified by:

the telecommunications system of the telecommunications provider; or

if the system used to transport the signal is not a system of the telecommunications provider, information received by the telecommunications provider form its service provider; or

if the locations described in Subsection (I)(1) or (2) are not known, the location of a customer’s place of primary use.

owns, controls, operates, or manages a telecommunications service; or

engages in an activity described in Subsection (J)(1)(a) for the shared use with or resale to any person of the telecommunications service.

that person; or

the telecommunications service that the person owns, controls, operates, or manages.

“Telecommunications service” means:

telephone service, as defined in Utah Code Ann. §59-12-102, other than mobile telecommunications service, that originates and terminates within the boundaries of this state and

mobile telecommunications service, as defined in Utah Code Ann. §59-12-102:

that originates and terminates within the boundaries of one state; and

3.40.010 Definitions

The following definitions apply to this Chapter:

"Person" means any individual, person, partnership, firm, association, corporation, limited liability company, limited liability partnership, or other entity.

"Public Transient Facilities" means a place providing temporary sleeping accommodations or temporary living arrangements to the public and includes, but is not necessarily limited, to the following:

A Hotel;

A Motel;

An Inn;

A bed and breakfast;

A trailer park offering Short Term Rentals;

A home, condominium, apartment, townhome, or other similar facility offering Short Term Rentals.

"Rents" means monies or other items of value exchanged for the right to use Public Transient Facilities for Short Term Rentals.

"Short Term Rentals" means a Public Transient Facility offering sleeping accommodations or temporary living arrangements for a period of less than thirty (30) consecutive days.

3.40.020 Transient Room Tax

There is hereby levied, on Persons occupying Short Term Rentals, a transient room tax of one percent (1%) of the value of Rents charged for the use of the Short-Term Rentals. This tax is in addition to any other taxes or fees to which the Short-Term Rentals are subject. Persons operating Short Term Rentals are responsible to collect the tax and remit it monthly to the Utah State Tax Commission.

3.40.030 Records

Persons operating Short Term Rentals shall maintain records kept in conformity with generally accepted accounting principles for the purpose of disclosing and verifying the gross revenues derived from providing Short Term Rentals subject to this Chapter. Such records must be maintained for a minimum of three years from the date services were provided. The records shall be available to Spanish Fork City or the Utah State Tax Commission for review and inspection during normal operating hours.

3.40.040 Penalties

Any person violating the provisions of this Chapter shall be subject a monetary penalty in the amount of ten percent (10%) of the amount due.

Sums due under this Chapter, and not paid in a timely fashion, shall incur interest at the rate of twelve percent (12%) per annum.

In addition to the monetary penalty, a Person or Short-Term Rental violating this policy is subject to having their business license revoked.

Failure to collect or remit the tax when due shall make the Person operating the Short-Term Rental personally liable for the sums due, including attorney’s fees and costs incurred in collecting the sums.

3.44 Disposal Of Lease Of Public Property

Surplus. Establish an equitable system for the transfer or sale of surplus supplies and equipment;

No public property having an estimated value in excess of one hundred dollars ($100.00) shall be disposed of or released to anyone other than the City, unless such property has been declared surplus by the City Finance Director. Fleet Division Manager may surplus vehicles and equipment approved to be replaced through the budget.

Whenever public property is surplus, unused, obsolete, unsuitable or otherwise no longer needed, the department head having control of such property shall notify the City Finance Director. The City Finance Director may notify other City departments of the availability of such property. The Finance Director shall supervise any transfer of such property to any other department. If no use can be made or can be expected to be made within the reasonably foreseeable future, the property shall be disposed of in accordance with this section.

The Finance Director shall prepare a listing of all City-owned property which s/he feels is no longer needed by the City and which can be declared surplus. After an item has been declared surplus, the property may be disposed of or leased. Except for vehicles and equipment budgeted to be replaced, if an item has an estimated salvage value over twenty thousand dollars ($20,000), the City Manager shall approve its disposal. All disposals, leases and/or subleases of public property shall be made, in accordance with law, under the direction of the City Finance Director.

Except as otherwise required by State law, boundary line agreements and deeds conveying unneeded portions of rights of way or easements may be executed without declaring the property surplus.

Before disposing of a significant parcel of real property, the City shall comply with the provisions of Utah Code Ann. § 10-8-2(4). For purposes of this section, a significant parcel of real property is defined to be any parcel with a value equal to or greater than $100,000.00. the City shall publish notice of the proposed disposition of the property on the Utah Public Notice Website, on the City's website, and post written notice on the property at least fourteen (14) days prior to a public hearing concerning the disposition.
